ABOUT THE PROJECT

This project was a part of the Information Architecture and Interaction Design course at University of California, Berkeley.

The Dream Center is a non-profit organization based in Los Angeles, California. Its mission is to offer hope and support to people facing challenges such as homelessness, poverty, addiction, and lack of education.

The goal of this project was to redesign the Dream Center website with a focus on the information architecture improvements and provide more transparency of the organization.

STEP 1: USER RESEARCH

We kicked off our project with User Research, in order to gain a clear understanding of Dream Center’s users. Our team discovered that the website caters to three distinct user groups, but our primary focus was on volunteers.

Research Techniques

I made use of the following research methods to understand users:

5 Interviews

We each conducted 1 semi-structured interview to understand user’s expectations and pain points of DC website.

44 Survey Responses

44 individuals unanimously responded to a survey we created using Google Forms. The aim was to understand individuals’ pains and preferences.

STEP 2: DEFINITION

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Prospective volunteers and donors face barriers due to trust issues, unclear opportunities, and balancing commitments when navigating through the Dream Center’s website. Our goal is to redesign the website by offering more transparency, information clarity, and facilitates a seamless balance between personal and professional commitments.

USABILITY TESTING PLAN

Target Users

Individuals who are looking for a meaningful way to contribute to their community and hope to find volunteer opportunities that align with their values.

Questions We Want to Be Answered

  • Will users be able to reach the Volunteer Opportunities page easily?
  • Will users be able to identify the search, calendar, contact, and login buttons on the home page?
  • Will users be able to find details about the "Our impact report"?

Key Learning

  • Create a path so the users can flow easily.
  • Change the icons and make it universal.
